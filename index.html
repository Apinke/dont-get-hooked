CHAPTER 1
What Just Happened?
🎣 Phishing is when a criminal pretends to be someone you trust — your bank, a company, even a friend — to steal your personal information.
⚠️ Urgency is the #1 trick. "Act NOW or lose your money!" is designed to make you panic and not think clearly.
🔍 Look at the URL: "b4nk-secure.com" uses the number 4 instead of the letter A. Real banks don't do this.
✅ Golden rule: When in doubt, open your browser and type the bank's real website address yourself — never click the link.

😈
I'll dress up as the bank and nobody will know it's me!
The criminal creates a fake website that looks EXACTLY like the real bank.
✅ REAL BANK
🔒 www.firstbank.com
Green padlock · Correct spelling · Official address
❌ FAKE SITE
🔓 www.firstb4nk-login.com
Strange URL · Different domain · Looks identical inside!
The fake site copies every colour, logo and word from the real one.
😨
It looks exactly the same! How can I tell the difference?
Amara is confused — the fake site looks perfect. But there IS a way to spot it!
CHAPTER 2
The Art of Disguise
🎭 Phishers are like digital costume artists — they copy every visual detail of a real website to fool you.
🔍 Always check the address bar at the very top of your browser. The URL is the one thing they cannot perfectly fake.
🔒 Look for the padlock icon — but remember: even fake sites can have padlocks now! The padlock means the connection is encrypted, NOT that the site is safe.
✏️ Common tricks: rn looks like m · 0 instead of o · extra words like "-secure" or "-login" · wrong domain ending (.net instead of .com)
📧CHAPTER 3: THE BAIT — EMAIL PHISHING
Phishing doesn't only happen by SMS. Email is the most common trap!
From: security@paypa1-alerts.com
Subject: ⚠️ Unusual Activity Detected!
Dear Valued Customer,
We have detected suspicious login from Nigeria. Your account will be CLOSED in 24 hours unless you verify immediately.
VERIFY MY ACCOUNT NOW →
🚩 RED FLAGS in this email:
❌ Sender: paypa1-alerts.com (uses number 1 not letter l)
❌ Creates urgency: "24 hours or CLOSED!"
❌ Generic greeting: "Dear Valued Customer"
❌ Asks you to click a link to "verify"
💡
Real companies NEVER ask for your password by email!
CHAPTER 3
Email Phishing Red Flags
📧 Check the sender's email address carefully — not just the display name. The real address often reveals the trick.
⏰ Urgency is manipulation. "24 hours!" "Act NOW!" "Account will be CLOSED!" — these are pressure tactics to stop you thinking clearly.
👤 Generic greetings like "Dear Customer" or "Dear User" are a warning sign. Your bank knows your name.
🔑 No legitimate company will ever ask for your password, PIN, or one-time code by email, text, or phone call.

Smishing = SMS + Phishing. Criminals send fake text messages too!

CHAPTER 4
Smishing — Text Message Traps
📱 Smishing is phishing via SMS. Criminals impersonate telecoms companies, banks, delivery services, and government agencies.
🎯 They target your fears: losing your SIM, missing a package, being arrested, having your account frozen.
🚫 Never click links in unexpected text messages. Go directly to the company's official website by typing the address yourself.
📞 Call the company directly using the number on their official website — not the number in the suspicious text.
📞CHAPTER 5: VISHING — THE VOICE CALL TRAP

Vishing = Voice + Phishing. Scammers call you pretending to be your bank.
😰

Panic makes people share things they never should.
✅ WHAT AMARA SHOULD DO:
💪🏾
I will NOT give my PIN. I'm hanging up and calling the number on the back of my card.
🔒 YOUR BANK WILL NEVER ASK FOR YOUR FULL PIN, PASSWORD, OR ONE-TIME CODE ON A CALL.
CHAPTER 5
Vishing — Voice Call Scams
📞 Vishing uses phone calls to steal information. Criminals sound professional, calm, and authoritative.
🧠 They create panic about your money to bypass your rational thinking. A panicking brain makes bad decisions.
🚫 Your bank will NEVER ask for your full PIN, full card number, or one-time passcode (OTP) during a phone call.
✅ Hang up and call back using the number printed on your bank card or the official bank website. Never use a number the caller gives you.

CHAPTER 6
AI-Powered Phishing
🤖 AI generates grammatically perfect, personalised phishing messages that are much harder to spot than old-style attacks.
📛 Your name, location, and recent activities can be found on social media and used to make messages feel personal and real.
🔍 You can no longer rely on spelling mistakes to identify phishing. Focus on the URL, the sender address, and the request being made.
✅ The golden rule never changes: go directly to the real website yourself. No matter how convincing the message is.

TWO-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (2FA) — Your second shield!
📱

Enable 2FA on your bank, email, and social media accounts RIGHT NOW. It blocks 99% of automated attacks!
CHAPTER 7
Passwords & Two-Factor Auth
🔐 Strong passwords are long (12+ characters), mix letters, numbers and symbols, and are different for every account.
🔒 Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) means even if a phisher steals your password, they still cannot get into your account without your phone.
📱 Enable 2FA everywhere: your bank, Gmail, WhatsApp, Instagram, and any account that offers it. It is the single most important thing you can do.
🗝️ Use a password manager — apps like Google Password Manager or Bitwarden remember strong unique passwords for every site so you don't have to.
🚨CHAPTER 8: IF YOU GET PHISHED — ACT FAST!
😱
Oh no... I clicked the link and entered my password. What do I do?!
It can happen to anyone. Acting quickly is what matters!
⚡ DO THESE IMMEDIATELY:
1️⃣ Change your password on the real website RIGHT NOW
2️⃣ Call your bank to freeze your account if needed
3️⃣ Enable 2FA if you haven't already
4️⃣ Check for any unauthorised transactions
5️⃣ Report the phishing site to the company it impersonated
💪🏾
I changed my password immediately. My account is safe and I reported the fake site!
Amara acted fast and protected herself!
CHAPTER 8
Emergency Response Plan
⚡ Speed matters. Every minute you wait gives the attacker more time to use your stolen credentials.
🔑 Change your password immediately on the real website — not through any link in the phishing message.
🏦 Call your bank if you entered financial information. Banks can freeze accounts and reverse fraudulent transactions quickly.
📣 Report it. Report to the company being impersonated, your national cybercrime authority, and warn your contacts in case the attacker targets them next.

CHAPTER 9
AI — Your Digital Bodyguard
🤖 AI security tools like Google Safe Browsing, Microsoft Defender, and Cloudflare Gateway scan billions of URLs to protect you.
📧 Email AI filters analyse messages for phishing patterns — sender reputation, link destinations, urgency language — before you see them.
⚡ Real-time blocking means AI can identify and block a brand-new phishing site within seconds of it being created.
🤝 AI + Human awareness is the winning combination. Technology helps enormously, but knowing what to look for is your ultimate protection.
